Introduction

The job market today is constantly changing, and understanding hiring trends can help both the job seekers and employers make better decisions. The project that we are doing analyzes a dataset of LinkedIn job postings from 2023 to 2024 to explore trends in job availability, industry demand, required skills, and hiring locations. As we observe shifts in the economy, new technologies, and the rise of remote work since the pandemic, it’s more important than ever to see how opportunities are evolving. By creating effective and informative visualizations of these trends, we hope to give students like us, professionals, and businesses useful insights and views into what the job market looks like today and where it might be headed. About the Data

Data Process

This website utilizes LinkedIn job postings from 2023-24.

Step One: Data Source

We sourced our data from the LinkedIn Job Postings (2023 - 2024) dataset on Kaggle. It comprises of 124,000+ LinkedIn job postings, offering a detailed snapshot of the job market. It includes job titles, descriptions, salaries, locations, application details, and work types. Company data covers descriptions, headquarters, employee counts, and LinkedIn followers. Supplemental files provide job benefits and required skills, making it valuable for labor market analysis, salary predictions, and industry trends.

Step Two: Data Size & EDA

To clean the data, we filtered out postings with missing values in key columns such as company name, salary details, job title, location, work type, experience level, and job description. We also focused on full-time positions with yearly salaries in USD. The clean dataset also excludes irrelevant or low-value columns and keeps postings with complete FIPS codes, resulting in 10,392 datapoints. We then enriched the postings with supplementary datasets regarding industry segmentation, skills, and geography.

Step Three: Final Attributes

The final dataset includes key attributes such as job and company identifiers, job titles, descriptions, and locations (including region, ZIP code, and FIPS codes). It captures industry classifications and associated skills, experience levels, salary details (normalized, min, and max), engagement metrics (views and applies), remote work allowance, and a direct job posting URL—providing a rich foundation for analyzing job market trends. There are 19 total attributes in the clean dataset.
