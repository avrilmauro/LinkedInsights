Introduction

In today’s dynamic job market, understanding the factors that influence a position's salary can provide valuable insights for both job seekers and employers who are navigating shifting industry demands, remote work trends, and emerging skills. Our project aims to explore how job salaries are related to different variables such as industry, location, required skills, job posting visibility, and applicant interest by analyzing a LinkedIn job postings dataset from 2023 to 2024. This analysis is especially relevant as students and professionals make strategic career decisions based on evolving economic and technological conditions, and salary tends to be a top factor of consideration amongst job seekers. To investigate these relationships, our tasks include cleaning and preparing the dataset, exploring trends through descriptive and comparative analyses, and creating both static and interactive visualizations. These visualizations will help us uncover complex patterns in the job market clearly and effectively to our audience: individuals who are interested in understanding the factors that affect job salaries in today’s employment ecosystem.
About the Data

Data Process

This website utilizes LinkedIn job postings from 2023-24.

Step One: Data Source

We sourced our data from the LinkedIn Job Postings (2023 - 2024) dataset on Kaggle. It comprises of 124,000+ LinkedIn job postings, offering a detailed snapshot of the job market. It includes job titles, descriptions, salaries, locations, application details, and work types. Company data covers descriptions, headquarters, employee counts, and LinkedIn followers. Supplemental files provide job benefits and required skills, making it valuable for labor market analysis, salary predictions, and industry trends.

Step Two: Data Size & EDA

To clean the data, we filtered out postings with missing values in key columns such as company name, salary details, job title, location, work type, experience level, and job description. We also focused on full-time positions with yearly salaries in USD. The clean dataset also excludes irrelevant or low-value columns and keeps postings with complete FIPS codes, resulting in 9,545 datapoints. We then enriched the postings with supplementary datasets regarding industry segmentation, skills, and geography.

Step Three: Final Attributes

The final dataset includes key attributes such as job and company identifiers, job titles, descriptions, and locations (including region, ZIP code, and FIPS codes). It captures industry classifications and associated skills, experience levels, salary details (normalized, min, and max), engagement metrics (views and applies), remote work allowance, and a direct job posting URL—providing a rich foundation for analyzing job market trends. There are 19 total attributes in the clean dataset.
