U.S. Interactive Job Sector Network (2023–2024)

This interactive network lets you explore how various sub-industries relate to broader job sectors. Use the dropdown menu to select a sector — the graph will update to show sub-industries within that category. You can zoom in and out and move the nodes around for better viewing at your desire. Each node's size and color reflect the average salary in that sub-industry: larger and lighter nodes typically indicate higher pay. This tool helps you quickly identify salary trends and opportunities across different parts of the job market.


Takeaways

This interactive network visualization highlights key differences in the structure and salary landscape across U.S. job sectors. Manufacturing & Industrial sector is observed to be the most diversified sector as they contain over 40 sub-industries which reflects how this sector is a foundational part of the economy and how it offers a wide range of career opportunities with salaries ranging from around $60K to over $220K. In contrast, Financial Services is more concentrated. It has fewer but higher-paying sub-industries like Investment Banking and Venture Capital, where average salaries often exceeds $140K and peak above $260K in Capital Markets. Technology & IT Services also shows a wide spread, with over 30 interconnected industries including software, cybersecurity, hardware, and data analytics. As expected, this sector includes some of the highest-paying roles (e.g. Space Research and Software Development) with salaries beyond $260K.
